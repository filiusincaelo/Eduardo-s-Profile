3.1. Analysis of Demographics Canada and Market Research#

3.2. Introduction#

Why this analysis? The objective of this study was to determine the population, its characteristics, and the ethnicity of Canada, with a focus on the Latin American population and customer preferences. Why Latin American? Because someone wanted to know where to sell their merchandise in Canada and the target market.

This is an analysis of the demographics of Canada, a study of the population based mostly on information from the Census 2016. This document will continue as follows:

3.3. Methods & Procedures#

To extract the data was used one of the files of the Census 2016, it was cleaned manually in excel and Python programming language was used to store, read the data and plot charts, main libraries used were pandas and matplotlib.pyplot.

Census 2016 Canada is the latest Census that was made in Canada and contains over 34000 rows and 14 columns with information of Canada and all provinces.

3.10. Conclusions#

  • In 2015, the age group with the highest population was between 18 and 60 years old. Today, considering factors like age and mortality rates, this range may extend from 18 to 64 years old.

  • The top 3 occupations with more people employed, were occupations related to 1. sales and services, 2. business and finance, 3. trades, transportation and equipment.

  • Income has been increasing over the past years due to inflation. By 2021, the median income was just over $40,000 CAD. Income varies across occupations, and statistics indicate a tendency for males to earn more than females.

  • The top four provinces with the highest Latin American populations are: 1. Ontario, 2. Quebec, 3. Alberta, and 4. British Columbia. In 2016, the total Latin American Canadian population exceeded 670,000, and it is believed that this number has now reached around 800,000..

  • Almost 9% of the population have diabetes, Statistics Canada believes nowadays around 3.6 million people live with diabetes

  • Beyond perceptions of healthiness, factors such as price sensitivity, availability, convenience, and personal values can significantly influence consumer behavior. However, adding features such as â€œhealthyâ€ and â€œorganicâ€ to a product can indeed attract the attention of up to 40% of Canadians, as indicated by the data showing that 38% associate organic products with being healthier. This suggests that emphasizing these attributes may be effective in appealing to a substantial portion of the Canadian population when marketing products
